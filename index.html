The last camellia

The novel simultaneously tells 2 stories, one of which took place in the 40s of the twentieth century, and the second - already in the new century. In 1940, a young American goes to England. A mysterious "flower thief" offers Flora Lewis a tempting deal. She should get a job as a nanny in the old estate of Lord Livingston. Having gained confidence in the owner of the house, the girl should quietly steal a rare variety of camellia. Once in the estate, Flora realizes that the old house hides some secret, perhaps even more than one. A year before the American arrived at the estate, Lady Anne, Lord Livingston's wife and mother of four, had died. The owner of the house died under mysterious circumstances. The Lord does not pay attention to his children and is not engaged in their upbringing. Flora becomes aware that in the 1930s girls were constantly disappearing in the vicinity of the estate. Miss Lewis wants to penetrate the secrets of the cursed house.

Back to you

The main character Charlotte is already sixty-five. She lives in Bermuda and does not expect surprises from life. Only every night she remembers the two years spent on a desert island with Gray - her savior and protector. Fate itself brought them together. Or rather, a terrible shipwreck that Charlotte got into when she went on a cruise with her young husband. But the robinsonade is over. Now the woman hides from reporters and quietly spends her old age. Charlotte's leisurely life is shattered when she discovers a glass bottle containing a message from Gray. Was he still waiting for her there on their island? But how to find the way to the beloved? Perhaps the journalist from the New York Times, who is so persistently seeking a meeting, will help with this ...

All the flowers in Paris

After partial memory loss, the daughter of a famous film actor, Caroline Williams, comes to Paris and rents an apartment on Rue Claire. Soon she realizes that the beautiful old house holds a gloomy secret. Karolina discovers letters from a certain Selina dated 1943 in one of the rooms. Selina was forcibly kept in the house, but she did not lose her presence of mind and did everything to save her loved ones, especially her little daughter. Dealing with mysteries, the roots of which go back more than half a century, Carolina finds the keys to her own memory, in the depths of which pain, faith, and love lurk.
